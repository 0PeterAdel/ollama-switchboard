# Code of Conduct

Ollama Switchboard is built for developers who want reliable local AI infrastructure. The community should be practical, respectful, and focused on improving the software.

This code of conduct applies to all project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation, commit messages, reviews, and any other official project communication.

## Our Standards

Expected behavior:

- Be respectful and professional.
- Assume good intent, but ask for clarification when something is unclear.
- Keep technical discussions focused on facts, tradeoffs, and user impact.
- Give actionable feedback in reviews.
- Accept constructive criticism without personal attacks.
- Credit ideas, reports, and contributions accurately.
- Be patient with contributors who are learning the project.
- Protect private information, secrets, and security reports.

Unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or personal attacks.
- Insults, slurs, or discriminatory language.
- Sexualized language or unwanted attention.
- Publishing private information without consent.
- Sharing secrets, tokens, private prompts, or private user data.
- Trolling, deliberate disruption, or repeated off-topic posting.
- Pressuring maintainers to bypass review, tests, or security process.
- Retaliation against someone who reports a concern.

## Technical Discussion Guidelines

Strong engineering disagreement is allowed. It should stay technical.

Good feedback:

- identifies a concrete problem;
- explains the risk or tradeoff;
- references code, tests, docs, or behavior;
- suggests a path forward.

Poor feedback:

- attacks the person instead of the change;
- dismisses questions without explanation;
- repeats a demand after maintainers have answered;
- turns a technical disagreement into a personal conflict.

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for:

- clarifying standards of acceptable behavior;
- applying standards consistently;
- editing or removing inappropriate content when needed;
- closing issues or PRs that become abusive or unsafe;
- protecting private security reports;
- taking proportionate action when this code is violated.

Maintainers may remove comments, close threads, block users, or restrict participation when behavior harms the project or community.

## Reporting Problems

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

1. Contact a maintainer privately when possible.
2. Include links, screenshots, or context if safe to share.
3. Do not include private secrets or personal data beyond what is necessary.

For security issues, follow `SECURITY.md` instead of using public issues.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers may choose one or more actions depending on severity and history:

- private clarification or warning;
- public reminder to keep discussion respectful;
- comment edit or deletion;
- temporary pause on an issue or pull request;
- temporary participation restriction;
- permanent ban from project spaces.

Severe cases, such as threats, harassment, doxxing, or intentional secret exposure, may result in immediate removal without prior warning.

## Scope

This code applies when participating in the project and when representing the project in public spaces. It also applies when behavior outside official spaces directly affects the safety or trust of the project community.
